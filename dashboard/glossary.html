3. ArchetypeUse RCV/OBR shape to separate damage teams from traffic teams.

All Formulas
ABQ = 30% Discipline + 35% Contact + 20% Pitch Pressure + 15% K Avoidance
RCV = 35% wRC+ + 32% Barrel/park + 20% ISO/park + 13% HardHit/park
OBR = 65% xwOBA + 35% BB%
OSI = 43% RCV + 37% ABQ + 20% OBR
projOSI = OSI + clip((xwOBA - wOBA) × 450, -8, +8)
OOR = 55% HvR + 45% HvL
PALS = (BA+ + PTF+) / 2

Metrics are pool-normalized to 0—100 within the active leaderboard sample. Park factors adjust barrel/ISO/hard-hit for home environment.

Nine Signal Definitions
Signal 1 — K% vs OBR

Read: Low-K arm against a lineup with on-base floor.

Use: Traffic, team total, and over context.

Signal 2 — BB% vs ABQ

Read: Command risk against a patient lineup.

Use: Walk/traffic pressure or pitcher edge if command is clean.

Signal 3 — HR/9 vs RCV

Read: Homer risk against damage quality.

Use: HR props, totals, and slug-heavy lineup spots.

Signal 4 — OSI vs Pitching Score

Read: Offense strength versus staff suppression.

Use: Primary matchup gap.

Signal 5 — PALS + projOSI

Read: Schedule-adjusted quality plus regression support.

Use: Buy-low or fade confirmation.

Signal 6 — OBR + BB%

Read: Table-setting against walk risk.

Use: Run creation paths without needing homers.

Signal 7 — ABQ platoon

Read: Handedness-specific plate-process edge.

Use: Lineup construction and SP hand context.

Signal 8 — RCV archetype

Read: Damage/traffic profile label.

Use: Decide whether the team wins through slug or base traffic.

Signal 9 — Schedule OOR

Read: Opponent difficulty versus baseline.

Use: Separate real form from soft/tough schedule noise.

Convergence: Fired signals sum weighted votes (PP-Gap counts double). Play flag at 4+ weighted convergence.
